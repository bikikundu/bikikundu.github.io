Associated Projects

  • In Silico Studies: An insight in modes of Holliday Junction DNA Binding by Human repair Helicase RecQ1
    May-July 2014

    The elementary project study involved the structural and functional aspect of RecQHelicases and the various modes of recombinations. Detailed study of various forms of DNA like tailed, blunt duplex, bubble, D loop, G quadruplex, duplex fork was done. An intricate theoretical study of Holliday junction was done in specific to recombination. The project comprised mainly of Bioinformatics and in silico structural biological studies using the computer softwares PyMol and Autodock. The initial experiments were done on RecQ Helicase of Ecoli and then on RecQ1 Helicase of Human. Docking of RecQ1 protein and Holliday Junction DNA was done to analyse the domain specific bindings and the influence of ADP in protein DNA Binding. The resulting amino acids which are involved in Hbonding with DNA backbone is subjected to mutations and docked again to study the change in the stability of the DNA protein complex thus deciding the amino acids contribution to the overall DNA Protein complex stability. Holliday Junction being a complex DNA Structure with four chains, it is difficult to analyse using existing docking softwares thus an alternative method to Docking was implemented in order to come up with results which involved superimposition of DNA with an already existing DNA Protein crystal structure and then subjecting to energy minimization simulations with the deletion of original DNA complex present in the crystal structure. Guide: Prof. Agneyo Ganguly, BT Department, IIT Kharagpur
